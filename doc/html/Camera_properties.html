Camera properties

Smartphone distortions, FOV, etc.

The distortions and focal lengths have been determined with: http://www.vision.caltech.edu/bouguetj/calib_doc/

The cameras have only small distortions. In fact they seem to have only higher order distortions. That is somewhat puzzleing. Maybe it is the calibration target that is distorted. The error is in the order of 1 pixel. So for now, we treat the cameas as if they have no distortions.

Notice how the calibration scales with the image size:
  • horizontal FOV is the same
  • vertical FOV scales with the width/height ratio: 4/3 vs 16/9
  • focal lengths scale with the width (not height!)
  • distortions remain the same
It then seems that the camera resamples the width and resamples and cuts the height to get a lower resolution image.

Smartphone extra lenses

There are a few companies that sell lenses that can be attached to smartphone. These lenses range from fisheye to tele-zoom lenses. Many of them are specifically for the iPhone. Others can be used for any smartphone. One brand has a mechanism where the lenses are interchangeable and attach with a magnet. Some reviews point out that the lenses tend to fall off. Another worry is that the magnet will mess up the compass.

Concerns with non-android digital cameras:

  1. Lots of time will be spent figuring out its API
  2. It might not be possible to remote control the camera with an android phone
  3. There might not be a continuous mode, only burst mode
  4. It might not be possible to geo-tag the HD movie
  5. There might be limited number of pictures (some say 230 pictures per charged battery)
  6. Geo tag and time stamps are low resolution (approx. 30 m and 1 min)

Biggest advantage of compact digital cameras:

  1. Higher resolution (16 Mp compared with 5 Mp)
  2. Zoom
  3. Instantenous shutter
