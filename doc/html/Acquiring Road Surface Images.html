Acquiring Road Surface Images

We experimented with two methods for capturing images of the road surface:
         1. Capture of still-images, using the RoadCam App on an android smart phone
         2. Recording of high-resolution (720P) videos at 10fps using RoadVid app (android 4.0 or higher) or at 30 fps using the android
             camcorder app on an android smart phone. Then each frame of the video is extracted and saved as still-images.

In both methods, the position of the phone is crucial to the property of the images taken. Height, orientation, speed, lighting, vibration, are all factors that contribute to varying quality images.

To test each method, we first captured the images by hand-holding the android phone at a height of around 1.5m(4'11'') and keeping it parallel to the road surface.
However, we discovered that at this height, the camera is too close to the surface and only a small area is captured.

in order to increase the area captured, we built a mount for the camera that can lift it above ground at 3m (10'). To avoid capturing the mount itself because of the increased area, the camera is placed 1 meter (3') away from the vertical pole. See the diagram below:

By adjusting the angle of the phone to the horizontal plane, we can increase the width of the image, and thus increase the area captured, but at the cost of a more distorted image due to the change in orientation.

The above apparatus was used on Forbes Ave, in front of Carnegie Mellon University in order to obtain photos for a segment of an actual road. We wanted to generate an image for a road segment that is approximately 50m long, and
covers the entire width of the road. The apparatus was held by hand, and we walked along the road from the starting point slowly toward the end point. Both still-images with RoadCam and videos with RoadVid were recorded
